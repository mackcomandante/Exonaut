

EXOASIA INNOVATION HUB

Exonaut Program Platform

Product Requirements &amp; Design Brief

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This document contains everything needed to design and build the Exonaut Program Platform — the gamified internship management system for Exoasia Innovation Hub. Read this fully before beginning any design or development work.

Prepared by Mack Comandante  ·  Founder &amp; CEO, Exoasia Innovation Hub  ·  Batch 2026–2027



1. Executive Summary

The Exonaut Program Platform is a full-stack web application that manages, gamifies, and celebrates the Exoasia Innovation Hub internship experience. It is the digital home of every Exonaut — from the moment they accept their offer through their entire post-program alumni journey.

The platform replaces spreadsheets, manual tracking, and fragmented group chats with a single, beautifully designed system that makes excellence visible, competition healthy, and culture contagious.



The platform serves three primary audiences:


Exonauts (Active Interns) — track their missions, points, badges, rank, and cohort standing in real time


Mission Leads &amp; Admins — manage cohorts, assign missions, award badges, post announcements, and generate reports


Alumni (Exonaut Corps) — maintain lifelong access to their profile, badges, and alumni network



The system is built around three pillars that every Exonaut must fulfill:


Pillar 1 — Project: Quality and completion of track deliverables and missions


Pillar 2 — Client: Relationship management and satisfaction score from their assigned client


Pillar 3 — Recruitment: Successfully identifying and referring the next Exonaut



The platform must be production-ready, mobile-responsive, deployable as a web app (ideally React + Node.js or a modern full-stack framework), and designed in Exoasia&apos;s brand colors.

2.3  Component Standards


Buttons: Primary = Lime bg + Deep Purple text. Ghost = Transparent + 1px Off-White border. Danger = Red border. Radius = 2px. Font = Montserrat ExtraBold 800. Always uppercase.


Cards: Background #3A1035 (default) or #3E1438 (hover). Border: 1px rgba(201,229,0,0.15). Radius = 6px. Shadow on hover only.


Inputs: Background #1A0516. Border: 1px rgba(245,240,248,0.15). Focus: 1px Lime border. Radius = 4px. Font: Montserrat. Text: Off-White.


Icons: Font Awesome 6.5+ via CDN (cdnjs). Always use FA icons — no emoji in UI components.


Borders: Hairline dividers at rgba(245,240,248,0.07). Accent borders at rgba(201,229,0,0.25).


Spacing: Base unit 8px. Component padding: 16–32px. Section padding: clamp(3rem, 6vw, 5rem).


Shadows: 0 8px 32px rgba(45,10,40,0.4) on elevated cards. Lime glow on primary CTAs: 0 0 24px rgba(201,229,0,0.22).


Animations: Entrance: translateY(20px) → 0 + opacity 0→1, 0.6s cubic-bezier(.22,1,.36,1). Hover transitions: 200–300ms ease. No jarring or bouncing.

4.3  First Login Onboarding Flow

When an Exonaut logs in for the first time, they are guided through a 4-step onboarding wizard before reaching their dashboard.




Step 1 — Welcome Screen: Displays their name, cohort, and track. Shows the Exonaut Pledge text. They must click &apos;I Accept the Pledge&apos; to proceed.


Step 2 — Profile Setup: Upload profile photo, write a 1-sentence bio, confirm their assigned track and client. All required.


Step 3 — Platform Tour: Brief 5-slide walkthrough of the dashboard, leaderboard, badge system, and mission tracker. Can be skipped but not before Step 2.


Step 4 — LinkedIn Prompt: Shows the LinkedIn announcement template pre-filled with their name and track. One-click copy button. Checkbox: &apos;I have posted my LinkedIn announcement&apos;. Required to unlock the full dashboard.

7.2  Pillar Weights

For Tier and special award calculations, pillar scores are weighted:


Pillar 1 — Project: 40% of total pillar score


Pillar 2 — Client: 35% of total pillar score


Pillar 3 — Recruitment: 25% of total pillar score

Raw total points (unweighted) determine the leaderboard rank. Weighted pillar scores determine tier upgrades and special badge eligibility.

9. Badge System

Badges are the most emotionally resonant part of the platform. They are digital certificates of achievement that live on every Exonaut&apos;s profile forever.




9.1  Badge Categories
Category
Description
Milestone Tiers (4)
Awarded when an Exonaut crosses a point threshold: Bronze Builder, Silver Strategist, Gold Innovator, Platinum Disruptor
Track Completion (7)
Awarded when the Exonaut completes all missions within their assigned track: AI Strategist, Venture Builder, L&amp;D Champion, Experience Maker, AI Dev Builder, Policy Analyst, Content Creator
Pillar Badges (4)
Awarded for achieving maximum score on a pillar: Delivery Machine (Project), Innovation Catalyst (Project), Client Champion (Client), Talent Scout (Recruitment)

9.2  Badge Display Rules


Earned badges: Full color, with name, date, and description on hover/tap

Badge ID format: EXO-BADGE-[CATEGORY]-[CODE]-[BATCH] e.g. EXO-BADGE-MIL-GLD-2026


Downloadable certificate: Each badge links to a downloadable PDF certificate (pre-filled with Exonaut&apos;s name, date, cohort)




9.3  Badge Award Triggers


Milestone tier badges: Auto-awarded when point threshold is crossed (system-generated)


Track completion badge: Auto-awarded when all track missions are marked Approved by Mission Lead


Pillar badges: Manually awarded by Admin or Mission Lead on the badge management screen


Special awards: Manually awarded by Admin only — usually at Demo Day


Intern of the Week badge: Auto-awarded weekly based on leaderboard logic, confirmed by Admin

12. Peer Kudos System

Kudos is the social recognition engine. It drives the &apos;lift before you climb&apos; culture and powers the Culture Carrier badge.




Any Exonaut can give kudos to any other Exonaut in their cohort


Maximum 3 kudos given per Exonaut per week (resets Monday)


Kudos are public — posted to the cohort activity feed with the giver&apos;s name and message


Giver earns +2 points. Receiver earns +3 points.


Kudos history visible on recipient&apos;s profile


The Exonaut with the most kudos received in the cohort earns the Culture Carrier badge



KUDOS FORM


Select recipient from cohort list (dropdown with photos)


Write a kudos message (max 200 characters, required)


Optional: tag a pillar (Project / Client / Recruitment) that the kudos is for


Submit button (&apos;Give Kudos&apos;) — Lime, prominent

20. Special UX Moments

These are the emotional high points of the platform experience. Build them with care — they are what Exonauts will remember.




Tier Upgrade Celebration: When an Exonaut crosses a tier threshold, display a full-screen overlay: their new tier badge animates in from center with a 3D rotation, confetti falls in Lime and Purple, the new tier name appears large, and a sound effect plays (optional, dismissible). This happens once per tier change.


Badge Earned Celebration: Similar to tier upgrade. The badge icon zooms in from the center, rotates, glows, and then settles. Shows the badge name, description, and earned date. &apos;Share to LinkedIn&apos; button appears.


Intern of the Week Announcement: When an Exonaut is named Intern of the Week, their profile photo appears large in the activity feed with a Lime glow animation, a star burst, and the cohort&apos;s reaction emojis visible in real time.


Rank Milestone Moments: When an Exonaut enters the Top 10, Top 5, or #1 for the first time, a brief toast notification appears: &apos;You just entered the Top 10! Keep building.&apos; These should feel earned, not cheap.


Pledge Signing: On Day 1 onboarding, the pledge signing screen should feel ceremonial. Large text, solemn dark background, signature field (stylus or type), Mack&apos;s signature pre-loaded. A &apos;You are now an Exonaut.&apos; confirmation after signing.


Launch Day / Program End: When the Admin marks the program as complete, every Exonaut&apos;s dashboard shows a special &apos;You&apos;ve Launched&apos; banner. Final badge certificates are auto-generated. A &apos;Your Exonaut Journey&apos; summary card is generated for LinkedIn sharing.

21. Important Notes for the Developer

Read these carefully before starting any work.




The brand guide in Section 2 is not a suggestion. It is the law. Every color, font, border radius, and spacing decision must match. If in doubt, ask before building.


&apos;GCF&apos; refers to Game Changer Funnel (GoHighLevel), which is Exoasia&apos;s CRM and website platform. The Exonaut Platform is a separate application but should be embeddable or linkable from GCF pages. Build as a standalone web app first.


Mobile-first is mandatory. Most Exonauts will check the platform on their phones. Every screen must be fully functional and beautiful on 375px viewport width.


The leaderboard is the most sensitive screen. Any bug that shows wrong rankings will erode trust immediately. Build it with strong test coverage and server-side rank calculation — never trust client-side math for rankings.


Points transactions are financial-grade. Every point award must be logged with source, awarded_by, and timestamp. No silent mutations. Build an audit trail.


Notifications must be near-real-time. Use Supabase Realtime or websockets. Polling is acceptable as fallback but not preferred. Exonauts should feel the platform is alive.


The certificate PDF feature is a premium moment. Every badge certificate should look like the PPTX certificates Exoasia already uses — same layout, same brand, same gravitas. Use the PPTX designs as reference.


Do not build for scale prematurely. Start at 30 Exonauts per cohort. Design the data model to scale to 500 alumni, but don&apos;t over-engineer the infrastructure.


The platform uses the same brand as the Exonaut Culture Guide landing page and the Exoasia Internship landing page. Maintain visual consistency with those references.


Every Admin action that awards or adjusts points must require a reason/note field. This protects integrity and creates an audit trail Mack can review.



When in doubt about any product decision, refer to the Exonaut Culture Guide and the Exoasia brand. The platform is an extension of that document — it should feel like it was built by Exonauts, for Exonauts.



We don&apos;t wait for the map. We build it.
